## About the Synixe SITREP

<img src="assets/img/synixsitrepimg/001img/sitrep001.webp"

This marks the first official Synixe SITREP.

Going forward, each SITREP will provide:

- A summary of our current theatre, campaign, or operational focus
- Monthly contract statistics and attendance averages
- Notable contract highlights
- Relevant structural or procedural updates
- A rotating member interview feature

Attendance averages will be calculated using After Action Reports from each contract. We will track:

- Average turnout per contract
- Highest and lowest turnout of the month
- Percentage change compared to the previous month

This system allows us to measure engagement without placing pressure on individual performance. 

## Operation Status 

**Previous Theatre:** Takistan  
**Operational Phase:** Concluded  
**Current Focus:** Standard Contracts   

<img src="assets/img/synixsitrepimg/001img/theincident.webp"

*The incident that preceded Synixe's dismissal from Takistan* 

With our time in Takistan coming to a halt, Synixe returns to regular contracted work. Our time in Takistan tested us, sharpened us, and at times pushed us further than expected. While we did not leave the theatre under ideal conditions, we left at the right time before things could get any worse. 

Our original plan in Takistan centered around advising local forces. On top of that, contractors focused on the disruption of hostile networks and stabilization efforts, as well as smaller humanitarian actions and village security. While the broader campaign did not conclude as we initially envisioned, several operations stand as clear successes. 

The campaign now being officially concluded, Synixe has returned to standard pickup contracts, deploying where needed and rebuilding momentum.

In the first contracts since, Synixe successfully extracted Leszek Nawrocki from the holdings of the Horizon Islands Government and safely reunited him with his mother. The week prior, Sadiq Naeem along with additional leadership figures of Ikhwan as-Sayf were extracted as well, effectively dismantling a major terror cell operating in the region. 

## Notes & Adjustments

The weekend of 2/28 and 3/1 marked the beginning of our reset.

As part of this fresh start, several contractor weapon platforms were limited, as well as a slight refresh and update to armors, helmets, and other headwear. Resets give us a clear opportunity to refine and rebuild deliberately rather than carry forward accumulated bad habits.

March will be about shaking off rust, reestablishing rhythm, and setting the tone for the coming months.

## Member Spotlight

<img src="assets/img/synixsitrepimg/001img/curio-image.webp"
     alt="Noah Curio"
     style="width:968px;height:auto" />
*Member – Noah Curio*

**What drew you to Synixe?**  
When I first saw the concept of Synixe six years ago, I thought it was so unique and instantly fell in love. The idea of gear and assets being persistent, having to think critically about what you bring or use, and the framework were all beautifully crafted.

**Which contract stood out this month and why?**  
Glasshouse. We were split into two teams and set up ambushes, waiting for an HVI to approach. Once secured, we had to double back to our boats and move quickly to extraction. Things escalated after a police unit investigated a missing vehicle, leading to a heavy firefight that pinned down both teams. The contract ended successfully, with the HVI returning safely to our FOB.

**What does “We win and lose as one” mean to you?**  
Teamwork is essential. As Brett Harrison once said, “You win as a team, you lose as a team.” That mindset builds trust. Trust in leadership and trust in peers strengthens the entire force.

**What are you looking forward to next month?**  
I’m hoping we can all shake off the rust coming into March, get our gear sorted after the reset, and see some new faces joining us for future contracts and possibly campaigns.
